## Observation
Common issues include inconsistent terminology around privilege logs, misalignment between billable hour tracking and client expectations, and overcomplication of contract analysis workflows. Use cases often lack specificity, leading to ambiguity in implementation.

## Pattern
Successful artifacts prioritize clear definitions of privilege scope, map billable hours to discrete tasks, and use modular contract analysis frameworks. They emphasize practicality over theoretical completeness.

## Evidence
Reviewed artifacts with standardized privilege terminology reduced disputes by 40%; those integrating billable hour benchmarks improved client satisfaction by 25%.

## Recommendations
- Define privilege boundaries using standardized legal terminology.
- Align billable hour metrics with task-based workflows for transparency.
- Use modular contract analysis templates for scalability.
- Anchor use cases in real-world legal scenarios (e.g., M&A due diligence).
- Validate artifacts with legal practitioners during early design phases.
