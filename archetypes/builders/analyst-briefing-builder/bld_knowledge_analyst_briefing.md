## Domain Overview
Analyst briefing artifacts structure vendor-to-analyst communications for Gartner, Forrester, IDC, and similar research firms. They provide the evidence substrate for Magic Quadrant, Forrester Wave, and IDC MarketScape evaluations, as well as one-on-one briefing sessions. An effective analyst briefing converts raw product and market data into a framework-aligned narrative that helps analysts accurately position the vendor in their research.

Analyst relations (AR) programs at technology vendors invest significantly in briefing quality. A well-structured briefing increases the probability of favorable positioning in research notes, can influence Magic Quadrant quadrant placement, and shapes the analyst's mental model of the vendor's differentiation. Poor briefings risk being cited as "incomplete vendor response" in research.

## Industry Standards
- Gartner Magic Quadrant Methodology (published annually per market)
- Forrester Wave Methodology (published annually per market)
- IDC MarketScape Methodology
- IIAR (Institute of Industry Analyst Relations) Best Practices
- Gartner Peer Insights (customer review validation)
- SiriusDecisions (now Forrester) Channel Program Frameworks

## Common Patterns
1. Open with a 150-word vendor overview: founding year, HQ, ARR, employee count, growth rate.
2. Map product strengths explicitly to Gartner/Forrester evaluation axes -- don't make analysts do the mapping themselves.
3. Include at least 3 numeric proof points per strength claim (latency, NPS, TCO, win rate, retention).
4. Prepare a Q&A section anticipating standard analyst probes: market differentiation, customer references, roadmap confidence, competitive response strategy.
5. Flag any roadmap content under NDA or embargo before the briefing.
6. Provide named customer references (minimum 3) for analyst follow-up.

## Pitfalls
- Generic claims without numeric evidence ("best in class," "market leader") -- analysts reject these.
- Conflating analyst briefing with sales pitch deck -- different audience, different purpose.
- Omitting competitive landscape -- analysts will ask; having no answer signals weak AR maturity.
- Missing or vague roadmap -- Gartner heavily weights "Completeness of Vision" on roadmap credibility.
- No NDA/embargo flag on forward-looking content -- creates legal and competitive risk.
- Claiming a Magic Quadrant or Forrester Wave position without citing the specific edition year.
