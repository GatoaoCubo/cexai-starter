# User Journey Map

The user journey represents the end-to-end experience a customer has with a product or service, from initial awareness to post-purchase engagement. This map helps businesses understand customer behavior, identify touchpoints, and optimize conversion paths.

## Key Stages

1. **Awareness**  
   The customer becomes aware of a problem or need.  
   *Touchpoints:* Advertising, content marketing, word-of-mouth.

2. **Consideration**  
   The customer researches solutions.  
   *Touchpoints:* Product comparisons, reviews, demos.

3. **Intent**  
   The customer shows clear interest in a specific solution.  
   *Touchpoints:* Website visits, email engagement, social media interactions.

4. **Evaluation**  
   The customer assesses options and compares vendors.  
   *Touchpoints:* Free trials, consultations, pricing comparisons.

5. **Purchase**  
   The customer makes a buying decision.  
   *Touchpoints:* Checkout, payment processing, order confirmation.

6. **Post-Purchase**  
   The customer uses the product/service and provides feedback.  
   *Touchpoints:* Customer support, loyalty programs, referrals.

## Purpose
This map helps businesses:
- Identify gaps in the customer experience
- Optimize marketing and sales strategies
- Improve conversion rates
- Enhance customer retention

## Metrics to Track
- Awareness stage conversion rate  
- Consideration phase engagement time  
- Decision funnel drop-off points  
- Post-purchase satisfaction scores  
- Net Promoter Score (NPS)

## Tools for Analysis
- Customer journey mapping software  
- Heatmaps and session recordings  
- CRM analytics dashboards  
- A/B testing platforms  
- Survey tools for post-purchase feedback
